## How to Pitch on a Sunday Meeting (Got Burger Style)

Based on the Got Burger pre-sales checklist, here's the meeting flow:

**Pre-meeting**: Send the scope document 24 hours before. Let them read it.
"Here's what I'm thinking for your project — take a look and we can go through it Sunday."

**Opening (5 min)**:
"Tell me about the restaurant — what's going well, what's broken right now?"
Listen more than you talk. Find the pain points.

**The pitch (10 min)**:
Walk through the scope doc. Show the demo of similar work.
Don't go feature by feature — go benefit by benefit.
"This means customers can order at 2am without calling anyone."
"This means you never miss a call during lunch rush."

**Questions to ask**:
- "What do you use for POS right now?" (Square/Toast/custom)
- "Do you have a logo file?" (determines if design work is needed)
- "Is the domain registered?" (determines setup work)
- "What's your phone number for the AI caller?" (signals they're serious)
- "Who handles your Instagram right now?" (opens retainer conversation)

**The close**:
"I can start this week if you want to lock it in. Deposit is $1,625 — that covers
everything up to launch. After launch it's $400/month for hosting, AI, and social."

**If they hesitate**:
"The website is 80% done. I built it before this meeting so you could see it.
The deposit just covers the remaining work and the launch."

**After the meeting**:
Send a follow-up email/DM within 2 hours.
Attach the signed scope doc and deposit invoice.
Include the demo video link one more time.
