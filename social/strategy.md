# Social Media Strategy — Agency Content

---

## The Play

Run this like an agency account, not a personal blog.
The angle is: **we build things for local businesses, here's the proof.**

No tutorials. No "how to" content. Just:
- Show the work
- Show the result
- Show the price
- Tell them to DM you

Every post is a sales asset disguised as content.

---

## Brand Positioning

**What you are**: A small team (even if it's just you) that builds AI phone agents,
websites, and automation for local restaurants and small businesses.

**Who it's for**: Local business owners — restaurants, salons, barbershops, clinics,
auto shops. People who are busy running their business and have money but no time
to figure out tech.

**What they get**: More orders. Less missed calls. A website that actually looks good.
Someone else handles the tech.

**Price anchors to show publicly**:
- AI Receptionist: $300 setup + $150/month
- Restaurant website: from $1,625
- Full package (site + AI): custom

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. Client Builds (70% of content)
Show what you built for a real business.
Format: screen recording or phone video of the finished product.
End with: the price paid and a DM CTA.

### 2. Behind the Build (20% of content)
Quick look at how something was made — the 3D model, the AI call flow,
the scraper running. NOT a tutorial. Just "look at this cool thing we made."
End with: "This is what we do. DM to get it."

### 3. Business/Process (10% of content)
The pitch doc, the scope sheet, the Sunday meeting questions.
Shows you're professional, organized, and treat client work seriously.
End with: a CTA to work with you.

---

## Content Format — VTS

Every short-form video follows this structure:

**V — Visual Hook (0–2 sec)**
The first frame stops the scroll. No intro, no "hey guys."
Start mid-action: the 3D burger already spinning. The AI already on a call.
The website already loading.

**T — The Show (2–15 sec)**
Show it. Don't explain it. Let the product speak.
Use text overlays for the key info (name, price, what it does).
Quick cuts. No filler.

**S — Sell (15–20 sec)**
One line. One CTA.
"DM me WEBSITE if you want this for your restaurant."
"Comment BURGER to see the full build."
"Follow to watch this project go live."

## Hooks That Work

Leading with the result:
- "This halal restaurant just got a full website + AI phone system for $1,625"
- "We built this local burger spot a 3D website"
- "This AI answers the restaurant's phone 24/7 and takes the order"

Leading with the tech:
- "3D model on a restaurant website — here's what that looks like"
- "AI phone agent for Got Burger, Ypsilanti MI"
- "This website loads in under 1 second — here's why"

Leading with the business:
- "How we landed a restaurant client with one meeting and one document"
- "Every local restaurant should have this"
- "We closed a $1,625 deal before building a single page"

## DM Keywords (set up auto-replies)

- **WEBSITE** → send your web dev pricing + link
- **AI** → send your AI receptionist pricing + demo
- **QUOTE** → send your contact info
- **BUILD** → send your full services list

---

## What NOT to Post

- Tutorials ("here's how to build a 3D website in Three.js")
- Overly technical breakdowns
- "Day in my life" personal content
- Opinions on tech trends
- Anything that doesn't have a clear client-facing angle

The account should look like a business, not a developer showing off.

---

## Platforms

**Primary**: Instagram Reels + TikTok (same video, cross-posted)
**Secondary**: LinkedIn (slightly more formal captions, same videos)

Film in 9:16 vertical. 15–30 seconds max. No talking head for now — 
screen recordings with text overlays and music are enough to start.
