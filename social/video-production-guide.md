# Video Production Guide — How to Film Everything

No camera crew. No studio. Just a MacBook and a phone.
Here's how to make every type of video in this playbook.

---

## Setup — One Time

**Screen recording**: Use QuickTime (Mac) or OBS (free).
- QuickTime → File → New Screen Recording
- Resolution: 1080p minimum
- Hide: bookmarks bar, notification badges, unnecessary tabs
- Use: Incognito/private mode for a clean browser
- Zoom in to 110–125% so text is readable on mobile

**Phone recording** (for your face or desk):
- Use the rear camera, not front (better quality)
- Find a spot with natural light hitting your face from the front
- Don't use the overhead light — it looks bad

**Audio**: You don't need to talk in these videos.
Most of your content is silent screen recording + text overlays + music.
If you do talk, use AirPods or a $20 clip mic.

**Editing app**: CapCut (free, best for this style)
Alternatively: Instagram built-in editor for quick posts.

---

## Screen Recording Videos (Most of your content)

1. Open QuickTime → New Screen Recording
2. Click record, select your full screen or just the browser window
3. Mouse movements: SLOW. Deliberate. Let things load.
4. For the Got Burger website: navigate slowly, pause on the 3D model for 5–8s
5. Stop recording, trim in QuickTime, export as MP4
6. Import to CapCut for text overlays and music
7. Export vertical (9:16) — CapCut has a crop tool for this

**Pro tip**: If your cursor appears in the recording, that's fine. 
Move it slowly and purposefully. Fast random mouse = amateur.

---

## Text Overlay Style

Use these settings in CapCut consistently:

**Headline text**: 
- Font: bold sans-serif (Montserrat Bold or similar)
- Color: White or Cyan
- Size: Large (fills about 30% of screen width)
- Background: none or very subtle shadow

**Sub-text / details**:
- Same font, lighter weight
- Color: White/60 opacity
- Smaller size

**CTA text** (at the end):
- Bold, large, centered
- Cyan colored
- "DM WEBSITE" or "DM AI" etc.

**Position**: Keep text out of the center third where the subject lives.
Top 20% of screen or bottom 30% of screen.

## Music

**Where to find**: CapCut has a free library with trending sounds.
Instagram has music built into Reels editor.
For TikTok — just use trending audio.

**What to use**:
- Chill lo-fi hip hop (for the "look what we built" tone)
- Trap instrumentals (for the "here's the price" reveals)
- Trending audio from TikTok/Instagram (algorithm boost from using trending sounds)

**Volume**: Keep music at 15–25% volume under any talking or AI demo audio.
For silent videos (screen recording only), music can be 60–80%.

**What NOT to use**:
- Songs with lyrics (distracting from your text overlays)
- Anything copyrighted for commercial use
- Generic "motivational" tracks (too generic)

## Filming the AI Receptionist Demo

This is your most important video. Film it perfectly.

**Setup**:
1. Open the Retell AI dashboard or your phone — whichever plays the demo
2. If playing from phone: hold it steady or prop it on a stand
3. If playing from computer: screen record with audio enabled

**What to capture**:
- The phone ringing sound
- AI picking up (first words)
- The full interaction: greeting → order taking → confirmation
- The order appearing in Square (if you can screen-share this too)

**Editing**:
- Add captions/subtitles so people can follow without sound
- Don't cut the audio — let the whole call play. 30–45 seconds is fine if it's good.
- Add text overlays that explain what's happening in real time

**Multiple cuts**: Film the same demo 3–4 times.
Use the best take. Or cut together the best moments from each.

## The "Terminal Running" Videos (For Automation Content)

Screen record your terminal while the scraper or automation runs.

**Make it readable**:
- Increase terminal font size (18–20pt minimum)
- Use a dark theme terminal (already dark, looks good on camera)
- Slow down the output if it's too fast (add logging pauses in the script)

**What to show**:
- The command being run (type it slowly)
- Results populating line by line
- The final output ("Found 247 businesses. Saved to leads.xlsx.")
- Then cut to: the Excel file opening and showing the data

## The "Before vs After" Videos

**Before**: Screenshot of a bad website (use a real local business — 
Google "[city] restaurant" and find someone with a terrible site.
Screenshot it. Add a big red X or just leave it as is.)

**After**: Your work (Got Burger, Hasmora, etc.)

Cut between them with a fast transition in CapCut.
Music drops on the "after" reveal.

---

## Posting Workflow

1. Record screen on Mac → QuickTime MP4
2. AirDrop to phone
3. Open CapCut on phone
4. Crop to 9:16 vertical
5. Add text overlays
6. Add music
7. Export
8. Post to TikTok → download the TikTok → post to Instagram Reels
   (Or post natively to both platforms)

**Timing**:
- Post TikTok first, then Instagram
- Best posting times: 12pm–2pm or 7pm–9pm local time
- Post consistently — same time every day if possible

---

## What Not to Do

- Don't film vertical on a laptop. Record horizontal, crop to vertical in CapCut.
- Don't use a mouse cursor that flies around the screen rapidly.
- Don't add unnecessary transitions between clips (hard cuts only).
- Don't add a logo watermark in the corner — it looks amateur.
- Don't put too much text on screen at once.
- Don't start with your logo or an intro screen. Start with the content.
- Don't use Comic Sans or script fonts.
- Don't record your screen with notifications enabled. Turn on Do Not Disturb.

## Quick Filming Checklist

Before every screen recording:
☐ Do Not Disturb: ON
☐ Bookmarks bar: HIDDEN
☐ Browser zoom: 110–125%
☐ Terminal font: 18pt+
☐ Mouse movements: slow and intentional
☐ Recording app: QuickTime (full screen)

Before every phone video:
☐ Portrait orientation
☐ Natural light from front
☐ Clean background (desk, wall, nothing distracting)
☐ Stabilize the phone (lean it against something)

After recording:
☐ Trim dead space at start/end
☐ Add text overlays
☐ Add music
☐ Check: can you understand this video with no sound?
☐ Check: is the CTA at the end?
☐ Export 1080x1920 (vertical HD)
